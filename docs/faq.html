What does Find relation do?

Find relation computes the relationship between two people using the PhD genealogy only.

Algorithm:

  • Resolve both names through the current dataset search matcher.
  • Build a directed PhD lineage graph: advisor -> student.
  • Check whether one person is on the other's ancestor chain.
  • If not, find the nearest common PhD ancestor and describe the collateral relation.

Examples:

  • Alice is Bob's PhD advisor.
  • Alice is Bob's academic grand-advisor.
  • Alice and Bob are academic siblings with the same PhD advisor.

What do the 7 badges mean?

avg coverage

Global average record completeness across the dataset.

Each person is scored over 7 checks: work.institution, undergraduate.school, masters.school, phd.school, phd advisor known, postdoc.school, postdoc advisor known.

same-school hires

Global rate of people whose current institution matches their PhD school.

Strict rule:

  • Must have work.institution
  • Must have phd.school
  • Must have phd.graduationYear != null
  • Must not be explicitly marked as a current PhD student

internal-lineage faculty

Global rate of current people who are inside a same-institution advisor lineage.

This also uses the strict graduation rule and excludes current PhD students.

unresolved profiles

Ranking-import seed profiles still missing core PhD-lineage evidence.

trees shown

Number of lineage trees that survive current filtering after removing components with <= 3 people.

schools

Number of distinct schools appearing in the currently displayed graph population.

lineage edges

Number of edges currently drawn in the active view.

What do the tabs mean?

Network graph

This is the family-level overview.

  • One node = one lineage family tree.
  • Postdoc bridge means a postdoc link connects two families.
  • Shared school means two families share at least one school.

Genealogy tree

This is the person-level tree for the family you selected in Network graph.

School affiliation filter

This is not a current-employer-only filter.

A person stays in the filtered population if the selected school appears in any recorded stage: work.institution, undergraduate.school, masters.school, phd.school, or postdoc.school.

Example: selecting MIT can still include someone who now works at Stanford if their PhD school was MIT.

Same-school hire ranking

This ranks schools by the share of visible people whose current institution matches their PhD school.

Columns: Rate, Same-school hires, Eligible people.

Internal lineage ranking

This ranks schools by how many current people are embedded in same-institution advisor lineages.

Columns: Rate, Internal-lineage faculty, Faculty with lineage data, Direct advisor ties.

School detail

This view appears after you click a school in Same-school hire ranking or Internal lineage ranking.

It shows nodes for current people at that school who survive the active filters, and edges for direct advisor ties where both sides currently work at that school.

The left-side School affiliation filter still applies first, so this is a drill-down inside the filtered population, not a global school census.
