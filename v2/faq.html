Genealogy lookup

What does Find relation do?

It uses PhD advisor links only. Postdoc and shared-school connections are not used for this result.

  1. Resolve both names against the current dataset.
  2. Build the directed PhD graph: advisor -> student.
  3. Check whether either person is on the other's ancestor chain.
  4. Otherwise, find the nearest common PhD ancestor.
Example: people with the same PhD advisor are reported as academic siblings. If no path is recorded, the result is not connected in the current PhD genealogy data.

Global signals

What do the completeness figures mean?

These footer figures are global. They do not change when a school filter or a graph tab changes.

Average coverage

The average share of seven recorded fields: current institution, undergraduate, masters, PhD school, PhD advisor, postdoc school, and postdoc advisor.

Missing PhD advisors

People with neither a linked advisor record nor an advisor name in their PhD stage.

Missing PhD schools

People whose PhD stage has no recorded school.

Missing current institutions

People with no recorded work.institution.

Family overview

How should I read the network graph?

Each node is one connected PhD lineage family. Larger nodes represent larger families.

Postdoc bridge

A directed link from the family containing a postdoc researcher to the family containing that postdoc advisor.

Shared school

An undirected context link between families that share a recorded institution. It is optional and does not determine family membership.

Select a family to see its incoming and outgoing postdoc bridges. Open Genealogy tree to inspect that selected family at person level.

Views

What do the five tabs show?

Network graph

Family-level navigation across PhD-lineage components.

Genealogy tree

Person-level PhD tree for the family selected in the network graph.

Same-school hire ranking

Schools ranked by the share of eligible people whose current institution matches their PhD school.

Internal lineage ranking

Schools ranked by faculty embedded in same-institution advisor lineages.

School detail

Interactive people-and-advisor-ties graph opened by selecting a school in either ranking.

Who is eligible for the school rankings?

A person must have a current institution, a PhD school, and a recorded phd.graduationYear. Explicit current PhD students are excluded.

Example: if 3 of 8 eligible people at a school work where they earned their PhD, the same-school hire rate is 37.5%.

Scope

How does the school affiliation filter work?

It is not limited to current employers. Selecting a school keeps people when that institution appears at any recorded stage: current work, undergraduate, masters, PhD, or postdoc.

Example: selecting MIT can include a professor now at Stanford if MIT is recorded as their PhD school. Rankings and school detail are calculated inside that filtered population.
